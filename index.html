Origins of Kundalini Yoga

Kundalini Yoga traces its roots back thousands of years to ancient India, where it was initially passed down orally from teacher to student. The term "Kundalini" refers to the primal energy believed to reside at the base of the spine, often depicted as a coiled serpent. The goal of Kundalini Yoga is to awaken and raise this dormant energy through a combination of physical postures, breathing techniques, meditation, and mantra repetition.
Over the centuries, Kundalini Yoga evolved and adapted, drawing influences from various Indian spiritual traditions such as Tantra, Hatha Yoga, and Vedanta. It wasn't until the 20th century that Kundalini Yoga gained wider recognition and popularity, thanks in large part to the efforts of prominent teachers such as Swami Sivananda Saraswati and Swami Vivekananda.

Yogi Bhajan founded the Kundalini Research Institute in 1969 and established the 3HO (Healthy, Happy, Holy Organization) Foundation to promote the teachings of Kundalini Yoga worldwide. He developed a structured system of Kundalini Yoga practices, including kriyas (specific sequences of exercises), meditations, and mantra chanting, designed to awaken consciousness and facilitate personal transformation.
Today, Kundalini Yoga continues to thrive as a dynamic and multifaceted spiritual practice, attracting practitioners from all walks of life. Its emphasis on holistic well-being, spiritual awakening, and self-empowerment resonates with individuals seeking deeper meaning, connection, and fulfillment in their lives. As Kundalini Yoga spreads globally, it remains rooted in its ancient origins while evolving to meet the needs of modern practitioners in an ever-changing world.
